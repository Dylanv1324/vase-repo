LESSON 1: The First Great Civilizations
----------------------------------------

What is a Civilization?
-----------------------
A civilization is a complex society with:
  - Cities and organized government
  - Written language
  - Art and architecture
  - Systems of religion
  - Division of labor (different jobs for different people)


The First Civilizations:
------------------------

1. MESOPOTAMIA (3500 BCE - 500 BCE)
   Location: Between Tigris and Euphrates rivers (modern-day Iraq)

   Achievements:
   * Invented writing (cuneiform)
   * Created the wheel
   * Developed the first written laws (Code of Hammurabi)
   * Built ziggurats (stepped pyramids)
   * Invented the calendar and mathematics


2. ANCIENT EGYPT (3100 BCE - 30 BCE)
   Location: Along the Nile River in North Africa

   Achievements:
   * Built the pyramids and sphinx
   * Developed hieroglyphic writing
   * Created papyrus for writing
   * Advanced medicine and mathematics
   * Mummification to preserve bodies

   Important Features:
   - Ruled by pharaohs (kings/queens)
   - Believed in life after death
   - The Nile River flooded yearly, providing rich soil


3. INDUS VALLEY CIVILIZATION (3300 BCE - 1300 BCE)
   Location: Modern-day Pakistan and India

   Achievements:
   * Planned cities with grid layouts
   * Advanced drainage and sewer systems
   * Standardized weights and measures
   * Created pottery and jewelry

   Important Cities: Harappa and Mohenjo-daro


4. ANCIENT CHINA (2070 BCE - 220 CE)
   Location: Along the Yellow River

   Achievements:
   * Invented paper, gunpowder, and the compass
   * Built the Great Wall of China
   * Developed silk making
   * Created beautiful bronze work
   * Developed Chinese writing system


Why Study Ancient Civilizations?
---------------------------------
* They invented things we still use today
* They solved problems that help us now
* They created art and stories that inspire us
* Understanding the past helps us understand the present


Common Features of All Ancient Civilizations:
----------------------------------------------
1. Developed near rivers (water for farming)
2. Created systems of writing
3. Built impressive architecture
4. Developed trade with other regions
5. Created organized governments and laws


Practice Questions:
-------------------
1. What are two achievements of Ancient Egypt?
2. Which civilization invented the wheel?
3. Why did civilizations develop near rivers?
4. What is cuneiform?

Answers:
1) Pyramids, hieroglyphics, papyrus, medicine (any two)
2) Mesopotamia
3) Rivers provided water for farming and drinking
4) The first form of writing, invented in Mesopotamia
